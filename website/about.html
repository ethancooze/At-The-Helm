About

We've been in it.
That's the whole point.

We didn't build At The Helm from a textbook. We built it from years inside businesses — running operations, fixing the unfixable, and learning what actually works when the pressure is on.

The advice is usually good. The execution is where it falls apart.

We've sat in the meetings where the consultant presents the strategy. We've watched the founder nod along. And we've been there three months later when nothing has changed — because the people who built the plan went home, and the people left behind didn't know how to deliver it.

That gap — between what needs to happen and what actually gets done — is where we work.

We come in, embed into the business, take ownership of operations, and stay until the job is finished. Not as an external voice. As part of your team.

The one holding it together at 11pm.

We're not going to tell you everything about how we work on a website.

The way we engage depends entirely on the business — the size, the stage, the specific problem, the team. Cookie-cutter approaches don't work in operations. Anyone who tells you otherwise hasn't actually done it.

What we can tell you is that every business we've worked with has left in a better position than when we arrived. Not just marginally better — structurally, permanently better. The founders we work with stop firefighting. The businesses we leave behind can grow without us.

Not every business is a fit for us — and we'd rather tell you that upfront than waste your time. The businesses we work best with tend to be founder-led, scaling fast, and at the point where operations have become the thing that's slowing everything else down.

If that's you, we should talk. If it's not quite there yet, we'll tell you — and we'll probably have a view on what to do instead.
