The work

Three businesses.
Three very different problems.
One outcome.

We've deliberately kept these brief. The detail is in the conversation — not on the website.

01
Consumer goods · UK

Supply chain stabilisation

A fast-growing business where growth had outpaced the supply chain. Stock issues. Constant firefighting. The founder spending half their week on problems that should never have reached them.

What we walked into

  • No clear ownership of the supply chain function
  • Suppliers managed reactively — always chasing, never planning
  • Stock issues were becoming a regular customer conversation
  • Founder still the person who knew where everything was

What changed

  • Supply chain fully owned and running without founder involvement
  • S&OP process implemented and embedded in the team's rhythm
  • Stock availability improved across all SKUs
  • Supplier relationships rebuilt on a structured, proactive footing
  • Operational infrastructure that could handle the next stage of growth
02
FMCG · UK

Founder extraction

The founder was the operations function. Every decision came through them. Every problem landed with them. The business had hit a ceiling — not because it lacked opportunity, but because it couldn't move without the founder in the loop.

What we walked into

  • No clear accountability structure in the ops team
  • Founder approving decisions that should never have reached them
  • Team unsure of priorities — everything felt urgent
  • No KPI framework — performance invisible

What changed

  • Founder fully out of day-to-day operations within three months
  • Clear ownership defined across every function
  • Weekly ops cadence established and team-led
  • KPI framework built, visible, and acted on
  • Business able to scale without founder as the bottleneck
03
Drinks · UK / EU / US

International ops build

A brand entering multiple new markets simultaneously. No operational infrastructure. No 3PL relationships. No compliance framework. A launch date that wasn't moving.

What we walked into

  • No supply chain built for the new markets
  • Distribution and fulfilment undefined
  • Compliance and documentation not started
  • Timeline pressure with no buffer

What changed

  • End-to-end supply chain built and operational before launch
  • 3PL and distribution partnerships established in each market
  • Compliance and documentation framework created and filed
  • Launch delivered on time, zero operational delays
  • Scalable playbook left behind for future market entry

Every founder we've worked with thought their situation was different. The chaos always looks the same from the outside. That's not a criticism — it's just what growth does to a business. We've learned to see it clearly, and fix it fast.

If any of that sounds like your business, it's worth 30 minutes of your time to find out what we'd do about it.
